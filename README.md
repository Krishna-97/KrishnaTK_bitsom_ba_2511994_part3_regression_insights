# Part 3: Regression-Based Business Insights & Model Interpretation

## Project Overview

This project applies simple and multiple linear regression techniques to analyze the factors influencing monthly sales across retail stores. The objective is to identify the key business drivers associated with sales performance and provide data-driven recommendations for management.

The analysis was conducted using Microsoft Excel Regression Analysis (Analysis ToolPak). The project includes data preparation, dummy variable creation, simple regression models, multiple regression analysis, model comparison, residual analysis, and final business recommendations.

---

# Business Problem

The retail leadership team wants to understand which operational and business factors have the strongest relationship with monthly sales.

The analysis aims to answer the following questions:

* Which variables have the greatest impact on monthly sales?
* Does increasing marketing spend improve sales?
* Does customer footfall significantly influence revenue?
* Does store type affect performance?
* How do discounts influence sales?
* Which regression model provides the most accurate explanation of sales performance?

The insights from this project support strategic decisions regarding marketing investment, inventory management, pricing strategy, and store operations.

---

# Dataset Description

The dataset contains monthly operational information for multiple retail stores.

The variables include:

| Variable                    | Description                                | Type               |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ | ------------------ |
| Store ID                    | Unique identifier for each store           | Identifier         |
| Monthly Sales               | Total monthly sales generated by the store | Dependent Variable |
| Marketing Spend             | Monthly marketing expenditure              | Numerical          |
| Footfall                    | Number of customer visits                  | Numerical          |
| Average Discount Percentage | Average promotional discount offered       | Numerical          |
| Store Type                  | Store category (Mall / Non-Mall)           | Categorical        |

The original dataset is stored in:

```text
data/business_regression_data.xlsx
```

# Dependent Variable

**Monthly Sales**

Monthly Sales is the response (dependent) variable that the regression models aim to predict and explain.

# Independent Variables

The following variables were evaluated as predictors:

* Marketing Spend
* Footfall
* Average Discount Percentage

# Dummy Variable Approach

The dataset includes the categorical variable **Store Type**.

To include this variable in the regression model, dummy variable encoding was performed.

### Dummy Variable Created

Mall Dummy

* Mall Store = 1
* Non-Mall Store = 0

### Reference Category

Non-Mall Store

Only one dummy variable was included to avoid perfect multicollinearity (Dummy Variable Trap).

---

# Regression Approach

Three regression models were developed.

## Model 1

Simple Linear Regression

Dependent Variable:

Monthly Sales

Independent Variable:

Marketing Spend

### Results

* R² = **0.167**
* Adjusted R² = **0.165**
* Marketing coefficient = **2.1296**
* P-value = **2.48E-14**

Interpretation:

Marketing spend has a statistically significant positive relationship with monthly sales. However, marketing alone explains only 16.7% of sales variation.

---

## Model 2

Simple Linear Regression

Dependent Variable:

Monthly Sales

Independent Variable:

Footfall

### Results

* R² = **0.736**
* Adjusted R² = **0.735**
* Footfall coefficient = **35.6780**
* P-value = **4.75E-94**

Interpretation:

Customer footfall is a strong predictor of monthly sales and explains approximately 73.6% of the variation in sales.

---

## Model 3

Multiple Linear Regression

Dependent Variable:

Monthly Sales

Independent Variables

* Marketing Spend
* Footfall
* Average Discount Percentage
* Mall Dummy Variable

### Results

* R² = **0.788**
* Adjusted R² = **0.786**
* Significance F < 0.001

The multiple regression model explains approximately 78.8% of monthly sales variation and provides the strongest overall predictive performance.

---

# Model Comparison Summary

| Model               | Variables                                    |    R² | Business Performance |
| ------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | ----: | -------------------- |
| Simple Regression 1 | Marketing Spend                              | 0.167 | Low                  |
| Simple Regression 2 | Footfall                                     | 0.736 | High                 |
| Multiple Regression | Marketing + Footfall + Discount + Mall Dummy | 0.788 | Best                 |

The multiple regression model was selected as the final model because it explains the largest proportion of variation in monthly sales while incorporating several important business drivers.

---

# Residual Analysis

Residual analysis was performed to evaluate model accuracy.

Residuals were calculated using:

**Residual = Actual Sales − Predicted Sales**

The analysis identified stores where the model:

* Under-predicted sales (positive residuals)
* Over-predicted sales (negative residuals)

Residual analysis helps management identify exceptional stores requiring further investigation.

---

# Final Model Selected

The Multiple Linear Regression Model was selected because it:

* Has the highest R² value (0.788)
* Includes multiple statistically significant predictors
* Produces better prediction accuracy
* Supports strategic business decision-making
* Explains sales variation more effectively than the simple regression models

---

# Business Recommendations

Based on the regression analysis, the following recommendations are made:

* Increase customer footfall through targeted marketing campaigns and in-store engagement initiatives.
* Continue investing in marketing activities, as marketing spend has a positive impact on sales.
* Review discount strategies carefully because excessive discounting is negatively associated with monthly sales.
* Focus investment on high-performing mall stores while investigating opportunities to improve performance in non-mall locations.
* Use the regression model as a decision-support tool alongside business expertise.

---

# Assumptions and Limitations

This analysis has several limitations.

* Regression identifies statistical associations but does not prove causation.
* Seasonal demand was not included.
* Competitor activity was unavailable.
* Local economic conditions were not considered.
* Customer demographics were not included.
* Promotional events outside average discounts were not captured.
* Future market conditions may differ from historical patterns.

Therefore, business decisions should combine regression insights with operational knowledge and market intelligence.

# Conclusion

The regression analysis demonstrates that **customer footfall** is the strongest individual predictor of monthly sales, while the **multiple regression model** provides the most comprehensive explanation of sales performance. Marketing spend contributes positively to sales, whereas higher average discount percentages are associated with lower sales after controlling for other variables. Store type also influences performance, with mall stores outperforming the reference category.

Overall, the multiple regression model offers valuable evidence for supporting strategic decisions related to marketing investment, customer acquisition, pricing, and store operations while recognizing that regression identifies associations rather than definitive causal relationships.
